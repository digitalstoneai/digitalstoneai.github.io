Project Summary

Client
Pacific Infrastructure Services, San Diego, CA. Regional inspection firm handling bridge, pier, and marine structure inspections.
Challenge
Manual drone and ROV footage review took 6-8 hours per structure and reports varied across inspectors.
System Type
Vision classification, defect mapping, severity scoring, inspector review, and structured report generation.
Contract Context
Workflows relevant to CalTrans, Port of San Diego, and defense-adjacent inspection environments.
Challenge

Inspection footage was slow to review and hard to standardize

The client needed a system that could accelerate review without removing expert oversight. Reports had to be consistent, searchable, and audit-friendly across inspectors and structure types.

  • Manual review load: 6-8 hours per structure before automation.
  • Quality issue: reports varied depending on who reviewed the footage.
  • Audit need: defect decisions needed confidence, severity, and override history.

Frame Processing

Different extraction rates for normal and flagged zones

  • Standard review: ffmpeg extracted 1 frame per second.
  • Flagged zones: extraction increased to 4 frames per second.
  • Database record: frame ID, location coordinates, defect type, severity, confidence score, and inspector override.
  • Human review: inspector confirms or overrides AI flags, and all decisions are logged.
